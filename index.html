Career

Career History: I received my BA in Creative Writing from Seton Hill University in 2014. Since then, I've worked various positions in the publishing field. I briefly worked as an editorial assistant for a romance publisher. Then, I was a production assistant for an adult business training and skill development company. After that, I was a contract editor of online course materials for a digital education company. For two years, I worked as an engagement specialist at the law firm K&L Gates. Before accepting my role at Pearson, I worked for four years as a technical writer/editor for Naval Nuclear Laboratories.

Current Role: In October of 2021 I started my new job as an editor for Pearson. Pearson is an education company. I edit assessment materials for all subjects/all grades K-12. I was also recently promoted to work as a Lead Editor of our Content & Language Supports program. This program makes accommodations to existing assessment materials for students in special populations (i.e. students who have physical or mental disadvantages or ELL students).

Goals: I'm very satisfied and excited about my new role at Pearson. It's hard to say what might come next, though, since everything is so new. I hope to continue to specialize in editing content for special populations. Making educational content more accessible and diverse is something I care strongly about. Maybe someday I will pursue a managerial role, but that is a long way away for now.
